Jurassic Snap

A VR Rail Shooter about snapping Dinos

Jurassic Snap is a VR Rail shooter about taking snaps of Dinos. I participated in this project as a Game Developer in a team of 3 people at the Dreamirl Studio, based in Toulouse.

Game Design

Provoke dinosaurs, take your best shots and bring them back to the professor to learn more about these creatures

The main protagonist of our game is a Young Celt, a woman overtrained to face the abomination living in the forest. Even overtrained she remains human, so we wanted the player to fight enemies that could kill her with pure strength, but also ones that could be killed efficiently with a precise blow, as represented in the David and Goliath myth. Our goal was to make the player feel proud of himself after beating each enemy, and even prouder if he took no damage during the process.

Thus, we imagined that enemies had weak points placed on their body and provided the player a kit of movement that allowed him to dodge incoming attacks if well-timed and hit weak points precisely. A spear seemed to be the best answer to our demands, providing both range and precision while keeping the thrill brought by close combat situations.

Retrospective

What I learned from this experience

My 6 months internship at Dreamirl showed me behind the scenes of an Indie game studio, allowing me to test my skills in a professional game making environment. Here some insights about what I learned from this experience :

  • Problems need to be raised as soon as they emerge. Some issues could have been resolved more efficiently by sharing them with the team in the earlier stages of the project.
  • Technology watch needs to be done before everything else. Several features were developed from scratch when plugins could have saved us a lot of time.
  • Build as much as you can. The goal was a release on Quest 2 but builds were made too late in the planning. Some features didnât work as expected on the device and optimization issues emerged too close to the release date.
  • Communication is key. Giving feedback on each others work highly improved group cohesion and the overall project quality.
